THE ARTIFICIAL RETINA

“Scientists Have Created an Artificial Retina Implant That Could Restore Vision to Millions”

Artificial Retina-Augus II

By: Tushar Gupta | Posted:24May2019

We all are aware of the robots and bionic body parts that has been trending in this era, but now the science has reached a new stage and has started working on "Artificial Retina" in order to provide vision to millions of blind people and hope that future advances in such a way that even more people can be benifitted from it. Last year such a device was approved by the FDA(Food and Drug Administration) named as Argus II, manufactured by second sight medical products. A human eye has approximately 1 million pixels of eye vision.

The Big Question is How it works?

It basically works on small electronic chip which is implanted onto the retina. The patient wears spectacles which contain a small video camera that wirelessly transmits images to the chip. Currently the resolution is limited to some extent of around 60 pixels, which can solve the problem of people with less or no vision, to read large letters, determine the location of moving objects or people and detect traffic onto the streets. However, it also noticed that with further advancement in chip technology it is likely to increase the level of vision.

Adverse Affects or Cons

In addition to having a limited number of pixels, the chip also lacks in the information processing that occurs in the layer of neurons in the retina. With majority being helped with it, there are some patients who have lost central vision due to age-related issues would not likely to be benefitted from Argus II because the human's peripheral vision is much better than the vision provided by the chip.

Future Implementation

As far the the advancement is considered Second Sight is planning to increase the number of pixels to 240 ina future prototype, that can benefit a major part of patients. Although the german company named Retinal Implant has begun testing with 1500- pixel device, yet to be judged and explored in the market. There are improvement in retina implants and various additional approaches are being used to restore the vision. One such approach is the use of Gene Therapy which is used to deliver light sensitive molecules to the retina. However it is still being tested in advance cause for human blindless.

Artificial Retina Implant Cost

The fact that electronic retinal implants are already approved for use in patients with retinitis pigmentosa and gene therapy for human blindness is in advanced clinical trials bodes well for future vision restoration or protection for people with retinal diseases.

The cost of the Argus II is approximately $150,000 additional fees include the implantation surgery and training to use the device.
